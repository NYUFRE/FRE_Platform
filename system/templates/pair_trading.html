Pair Trading

Pair trading is a well-known statistical arbitrage trading strategy for stock trading. An an example, stock pairs are selected from S&P500 technology sector based on OLS regression, cointegration test and ADF test.

Pair Selection

1) The historical daily price data from 2019-01-01 to 2020-07-31 are used for stock pair selection

2) A pair of stocks are selected if their correlation >= 0.95 and ADF p_value <= 0.01

Pair Trading Logic

1) Open Spread: St = ln(stock 1 open price) - β0 - β1 * ln(stock 2 open price)

2) Close Spread: St = ln(stock 1 close price) - β0 - β1 * ln(stock 2 close price)

3) Z score: Zt = (St - μt) / σt

4) Open Trades:
    • Open short position if Zt >= threshold, sell stock 1 and buy stock 2
    • Open long position if Zt <= -threshold, buy stock 1 and sell stock 2

5) Close Trades:
    • Close the open positions for each pair at market close

Back Testing

Use the daily price data from 2020-08-01 to 2020-09-15 for pair trading and P&L calculation

Probation Testing

Any two trading dates before current date could be used for conducting pair trading
