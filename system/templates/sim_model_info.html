Bollinger Bands Trading Model


Bollinger Bands (BBs) are a type of price envelope developed by John Bollinger. (Price envelopes define upper and lower price range levels.) Bollinger Bands are envelopes plotted at a standard deviation level above and below a simple moving average of the price. Because the distance of the bands is based on standard deviation, they adjust to volatility swings in the underlying price.

Stock Selection

Within S&P500 component pool, select 4 stocks with highest 1-minute volatility over the last month of the historical data part.

Reason: 1. S&P 500 components are with large size, high liquidity. 2. The volatility among minutes for one-month intraday price data are checked for generating trading signals) 3. Higher volatility may trigger signals more often, more signals can lead better test the efficacy of trading model (i.e. training params)

Bollinger Bands Trading Logic

1) The H-period simple moving average (SMA). This serves as both the center of the BBs, and the baseline for determining the location of the two bands.

2) Up : The upper band line that is k1 (default is 2) standard deviation from the H-minute SMA

3) Down : The lower band line that is k1 standard deviation from the H-period SMA

4) Open Trades:
    • Open short position if Close Price >= Up, sell the stock
    • Open long position if Close Price <= Down, buy the stock

5) Close Trades:
    • Close the open positions when Close Price return within Up and Down
